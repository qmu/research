# Branch story — work-20260713-103908

## 1. Overview

This branch applies the owner's six editorial demands from reviewing the v0.1.0 reflection onto qmu-co-jp, fixing every issue at its root in this repository (the canonical source) so the corrections propagate through the normal publish-ticket flow. It also retires two stale standing tickets whose work shipped with PR #15.

**Highlights:**

1. Title-equals-sidebar-label is now a machine-checked policy: every published page (English and Japanese) carries its sidebar label as frontmatter title and H1, enforced by a disk-reading unit test, and the translation stage forces it on regeneration
2. The Japanese surface is properly titled 「LLM基礎検証」 and six topics get concise Japanese names (対象モデル・応答速度・出力精度・API可用性・OCR能力・ベクトルDBの比較) with URLs unchanged

5. The speed and accuracy articles lead with a concise best/median/worst overview in §4; the exhaustive per-configuration tables moved to §7, and both Japanese reports were regenerated

## 2. Motivation

The owner reviewed what v0.1.0 published to qmu.co.jp and filed six demands (2026-07-13): the sidebar group must follow the site's 「<テーマ>について」 convention, the JP index surfaced the placeholder title "LLMs Research (Japanese)", topic labels were verbose, mermaid is unsupported on the corporate renderer, the llm-benchmark seed is not publication-grade, and the speed/accuracy articles buried the takeaway under per-model tables. Root-cause analysis attributed the title drift to this repository — the publish ticket specified file order but not human-facing titles — so the fix is to make titles, group label, and deletions explicit, machine-checked outputs of the shared metadata rather than qmu-co-jp-side guesses.

## 7. Successful Development Patterns

- **Encode the hand-off, not just the artifact:** the v0.1.0 defect existed because the publish ticket omitted human-facing titles; making titles, group label, and deletions explicit payload fields turns an editorial convention into a machine-applied instruction ([c3011f5](https://github.com/qmu/research/commit/c3011f5))
- **Guard tests read the committed files:** asserting policies over what is actually on disk (not just renderer output) catches hand-edit drift, stale regenerations, and translation-stage regressions with one test ([c3011f5](https://github.com/qmu/research/commit/c3011f5))
- **Force deterministic fields through non-deterministic stages:** the LLM translation now has its title/H1 overwritten deterministically after translation, so a label policy never depends on model behavior ([c3011f5](https://github.com/qmu/research/commit/c3011f5))
