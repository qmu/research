# Benchmark how well AI systems keep up with real-world change

## Direction

Clients repeatedly ask which AI systems actually know what is happening *right now* —
not what a model memorized at training time, but what it can retrieve and correctly
report about the world as events unfold. This strategy keeps qmu's guidance on that
question evidence-based: it maintains a recurring, reproducible, publicly auditable
measurement of how well search-augmented AI products track real-world change —
correctness on recent events, citation freshness and validity, and the latency and cost
of staying current — with paired ungrounded controls that isolate live retrieval from
parametric memory.

The direction is comparative and continuous by design: both the world and the products
move, so the useful answer is never a one-time ranking but who is *keeping up* over time.
Missions under this strategy build and refresh the instruments that produce that answer
(the `trend-recency` topic first, and any later capability that speaks to real-time
knowledge). There is no completion condition — as long as clients depend on AI systems to
reflect a changing world, this measurement stays worth running.
