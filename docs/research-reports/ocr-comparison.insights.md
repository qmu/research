On this two-document synthetic OCR fixture, three Anthropic models — Claude Fable 5, Claude Opus 4.8, and Claude Sonnet 5 — reached zero character-error-rate and zero word-error-rate, with perfect (1.0) field-extraction accuracy across all six schema fields on both the receipt and invoice documents. Claude Haiku 4.5 was the only measured model to show any error: a mean CER of about 0.011, a mean WER of about 0.051, and field accuracy of 0.833 (5/6 fields), driven by a misread vendor name on the receipt ("OMU OFFICE SUPPLY" instead of "KANDA STATIONERY") and a transposed document ID on the invoice ("IMU-2026-0142" instead of "INV-2026-0142"). All other listed models (OpenAI's GPT-5.x/o4-mini family, Google's Gemini 3.x family, and xAI's Grok models) are marked "out-of-scope" with zero trials, failing with "No VisionClient adapter is registered for this provider/API surface" — these are integration gaps, not measured accuracy floors, and should not be read as OCR failures.

The practical takeaway is narrow: among models this harness could actually run, the larger/flagship Claude tiers transcribed and extracted structured fields flawlessly on clean, machine-rendered synthetic text (5x7 bitmap font, no skew or noise), while the smaller Haiku tier showed measurable but modest degradation concentrated in proper nouns and alphanumeric IDs — exactly the tokens most sensitive to visual ambiguity (e.g., "N" vs "M", "K" vs "Q/O"). This suggests a capability/cost trade-off within the Claude line: Haiku's lower per-call cost and (per the elapsedMs in the calls) faster response times (roughly 6–11s vs Sonnet/Opus/Fable's 5–15s range) come with a small but non-zero risk to exact-match field accuracy, which matters for downstream fields like vendor names or document IDs where fuzzy-correct isn't good enough.

These results should not be over-generalized. The fixture is a single deterministic, repository-authored synthetic corpus (two documents, one trial per model) rendered at 150 DPI with a fixed bitmap font, no rotation, blur, handwriting, stamps, or overlapping layout — conditions considerably easier than real-world scanned documents. With n=2 documents per model and only 1 trial requested, the stdDev values of 0 (for the three perfect models) reflect an absence of variance in this tiny sample rather than a guarantee of zero error at scale; Haiku's non-zero stdDev on CER/WER likewise comes from just two data points. There is no real-image OCR corpus in this revision, so none of these numbers should be extrapolated to handwriting, noisy scans, non-Latin scripts, or multi-page documents.

Finally, roughly half of the models nominally covered by this comparison (all OpenAI, Google, and xAI entries) produced no usable OCR data at all due to a missing vision-client adapter, not due to poor accuracy — a reader selecting on this artifact alone can only compare within the Anthropic set, and should treat the OpenAI/Google/xAI absence as an infrastructure/integration gap to be closed before any cross-vendor accuracy claim can be made. Until that adapter coverage exists and a real-image dataset is added with more trials, this artifact supports only a limited, provisional conclusion: flagship Claude models handle this clean synthetic layout without measurable transcription or field error, while the smaller Haiku model trades some field-level precision for its faster response time.
